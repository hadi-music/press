Artist Bio

Hadi is a South-Lebanese-based artist and producer working across Arabic rap, experimental sound, and conceptual design. His work approaches music as a system rather than a performance, prioritizing structure, repetition, and long-form coherence over immediacy. Interested in anxiety and pressure as both subject and material, he builds projects that sit between documentation and transformation. His current work focuses on rethinking how Arabic music can function — sonically, visually, and structurally — without asking for permission.

About the Project

Al-Fam wal-Fakkayn is a conceptual Arabic rap album that examines war through jaw anatomy, using structure, repetition, and restraint to trace how pressure reshapes the voice. The core tension sits between voice and structure. The jaw appears not as a metaphor, but as a mechanism. Language is not presented as self-expression, but as labor — something the body performs under conditions it did not design. Rather than functioning as a collection of songs, the album operates as a system, where pressure, looping, and restraint function as method. Each track tests how much distortion meaning can tolerate before it begins to malfunction. Hadi is not trying to fit into the Arabic scene. He is trying to change how it behaves. If the music feels restrained, that’s intentional. If it feels obsessive, that’s accurate. If this sounds controlled, it’s because losing control would be less interesting.
